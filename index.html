ParrotTTS: Text-to-Speech synthesis by exploiting self-supervised representations

Abstract:

Text-to-speech (TTS) systems are modelled as mel-synthesizers followed by speech-vocoders since the era of statistical TTS that is carried forward into neural designs. These data-hungry models train end-to-end on ample paired (speech-text) samples for all voices of interest. We take an alternative approach borrowing acoustic modeling from self-supervised learning (SSL) methods and decoupling it from learning content mapping. This with two-phase training of the acoustic and content-mapper independently has significantly reduced the paired-data requirement. We present extensive results with this model, that we refer to as~\ourmodel, showing that it is competitive to or improves upon state-of-the-art under various settings. There are quality improvements in multi-speaker models and preservation of speaker traits when rendering in non-native language. We observe prominent gains in zero-shot setting with \ourmodel~generalising well to novel languages the acoustic model is not trained on. This comes with significant data efficiency enabling easier expansion to low-resource languages.
